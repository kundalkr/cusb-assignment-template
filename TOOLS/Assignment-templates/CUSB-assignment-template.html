Central University of South Bihar (CUSB): A Hub of Learning and Growth

Nestled in the historic city of Gaya, the Central University of South Bihar (CUSB) has emerged as one of the leading central universities in India. Established under the Central Universities Act, 2009, it was initially known as the Central University of Bihar (CUB). A few years later, in 2014, the name was officially changed to Central University of South Bihar through the Central Universities (Amendment) Act. Today, CUSB stands tall among the 54 central universities governed by the Ministry of Education, Government of India.

A Campus that Inspires

The CUSB campus is more than just a space for classrooms—it’s a thoughtfully designed environment that blends modern infrastructure with academic excellence. Each building on the campus carries a unique identity and purpose:

  • Aryabhatta Bhawan - Home to the School of Earth, Biological & Environmental Sciences.
  • Chanakya Bhawan - Dedicated to the School of Social Sciences & Policy.
  • Malaviya Bhawan - Housing the School of Education.

Beyond these schools, the campus also boasts the Vivekanand Lecture Complex, Sangharam Guest House, and well-designed residential facilities for students. The Boys’ Hostel (Vinayak Damodar Sawarkar Block A & B) and the Girls’ Hostels (Maitreyi Sadan and Gargi Sadan) provide a safe and welcoming atmosphere, making campus life vibrant and supportive.

Why CUSB Matters ?

CUSB is not just an academic institution; it is a growing hub of research, cultural exchange, and personal development. With its diverse programs and student-friendly environment, it has become a preferred destination for learners from across Bihar and beyond. The serene surroundings of Gaya, combined with world-class infrastructure, make it an ideal place for education and innovation.
